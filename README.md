# Hotel-Booking-Analysis-Dashboard
## Key Findings

- Total bookings are **6,797**, with **1,191 cancellations**, showing a noticeable cancellation rate.
- **Check-out** status dominates the bookings, while **no-shows** are very low.
- **City Hotel** has a higher **Average Daily Rate ($78.51)** than **Resort Hotel ($53.94)**.
- The **Corporate** segment contributes the highest number of bookings across both hotel types.
- **Resort Hotel** performs better in **group bookings** compared to City Hotel.
- **Weeknight stays** are higher than weekend stays, indicating stronger business travel demand.
- **Adults** make up the majority of guests, while children and babies account for a very small share.
- **Parking demand** is higher for City Hotel than Resort Hotel.
- Room Type **A** shows the highest guest allocation, making it the most frequently assigned room type.

## Business Recommendations

- Introduce improved cancellation and deposit policies to reduce booking losses.
- Strengthen corporate partnerships, as corporate travelers form the largest customer segment.
- Increase direct bookings through exclusive offers and loyalty benefits.
- Launch weekend promotional packages to improve weekend occupancy.
- Apply dynamic pricing strategies based on hotel type and seasonal demand.
- Focus more group and event-based packages on Resort Hotel.
- Improve parking management and convenience, especially for City Hotel guests.
- Build repeat guest retention strategies through personalized offers and loyalty programs.

## Conclusion

This dashboard shows that hotel performance is mainly driven by corporate bookings, higher weeknight demand, and stronger ADR performance in City Hotel. At the same time, cancellations and lower weekend demand highlight areas for improvement. With better pricing, booking policies, customer targeting, and retention strategies, both occupancy and revenue performance can be improved.
